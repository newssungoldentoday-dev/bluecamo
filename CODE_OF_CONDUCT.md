# Bluecamo Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Bluecamo is built as an open, undercover-friendly social space. We want everyone —
contributors, maintainers, and users — to feel safe participating, whether you're
posting on the platform or contributing code to the repository. This Code of
Conduct applies to both the **GitHub project** (issues, pull requests, discussions)
and the **Bluecamo platform itself** (posts, comments, messages).

## Respect and Kindness

- Treat others the way you'd want to be treated — assume good faith before
  assuming bad intent.
- Disagreement is fine. Disrespect isn't. Critique ideas and code, not people.
- No profanity, vulgar language, or slurs directed at another person, group,
  or community.
- No harassment, doxxing, stalking, or unwanted contact of any kind.
- No hate speech, discrimination, or content that demeans someone based on
  race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, gender, sexual orientation,
  disability, or age.

## Expected Behavior

- Be constructive in code reviews, issues, and pull requests.
- Give credit where it's due — don't pass off others' work as your own.
- Keep platform content lawful. Don't post content that infringes on
  copyright, incites violence, or violates another user's privacy.
- Report bugs and security issues responsibly (see below) instead of
  exploiting them.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, or intimidation — in issues, PRs, DMs, or on-platform.
- Sharing another person's private information without consent
  (in line with the Philippine Data Privacy Act, RA 10173).
- Spam, scams, or coordinated inauthentic behavior on the platform.
- Submitting malicious code, exploits, or deliberately harmful pull requests.
- Impersonating another user, contributor, or maintainer.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All Bluecamo repository spaces (issues, PRs, discussions, commits).
- All Bluecamo platform spaces (posts, comments, messages, profiles).
- Any Bluecamo-related communication in official channels.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness a violation:

1. Open a private report via GitHub by contacting a maintainer directly, or
2. Use the platform's report feature (once available) for on-platform issues.

Reports are reviewed by maintainers and handled confidentially. We will not
share the identity of a reporter without their consent, except where required
by law.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

- A private warning.
- Temporary or permanent suspension from the repository (issues/PRs) or the
  platform (posting/commenting privileges).
- Removal of offending content.
- Reporting to relevant authorities where the law requires it.

Enforcement decisions are made at maintainer discretion, aiming for
proportionate and fair outcomes.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted for the Bluecamo project and platform, drawing
on common open-source community standards and the Philippine Data Privacy Act
(RA 10173) for privacy-related provisions.
